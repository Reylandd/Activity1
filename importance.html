Importance


The importance of this law is that it recognizes the potential for positive change in young offenders and seeks to transform them into responsible and productive members of society. It also adheres to the international standards of child protection, especially those to which the Philippines is a party. It promotes the best interests of the child, the participation of the child, and the respect for the dignity and worth of the child. It also adopts measures for dealing with children without resorting to judicial proceedings, whenever appropriate and desirable. It provides a variety of disposition measures that are child-appropriate, such as care, guidance, supervision, counseling, probation, foster care, education and vocational training programs and other alternatives to institutional care.
